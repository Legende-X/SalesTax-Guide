Consumption Tax Guide
for Retirees Abroad

What you actually pay in VAT and sales tax across 8 top retirement destinations.

When budgeting for retirement abroad, most people focus on rent and groceries — and miss the consumption tax layer underneath every purchase. A 23% VAT on clothing in Portugal or a 24% rate in Greece adds up fast. This table shows you the actual tax rate applied to each spending category in each country, so your budget numbers are real.

Important: Malaysia uses a Sales & Service Tax (SST) system rather than VAT. Costa Rica and Vietnam use VAT with multiple rates. Thailand's standard VAT is 7% (extended to September 2026). All rates reflect the latest available legislation as of mid-2025 to early 2026.

Country Notes & Important Nuances

🇵🇹 Portugal

Three VAT tiers: 6% (super-reduced), 13% (intermediate), 23% (standard). Electricity is 6% for a base consumption allowance, then 23% above it. Residential rent is fully exempt. Telecom and internet are taxed at the full 23%. Healthcare from licensed professionals is exempt.

🇬🇷 Greece

Three VAT tiers: 6%, 13%, 24%. Food, hotels and restaurants are at 13%. Electricity and gas are at the super-reduced 6%. Electronics and clothing at the full 24%. Healthcare services provided by licensed medical professionals are exempt. Long-term residential rent is exempt.

🇪🇸 Spain

Three tiers: 4% (super-reduced), 10% (reduced), 21% (standard). Basic foods returned to 4% from January 2025. Restaurants are 10%. Electricity returned to the full 21% from January 2025 (was temporarily reduced). Water is 10%. Healthcare and long-term residential rent are exempt.

🇲🇾 Malaysia

Uses Sales & Service Tax (SST), not VAT. Essential foods, medicines and basic goods are 0%. Electronics, clothing and furniture are 10% sales tax. Food & beverage services and telecoms are 6% service tax. Hotels and most services are 8% service tax. Private healthcare is 6% SST from July 2025.

🇨🇷 Costa Rica

Standard VAT is 13%. Basic staple foods (unprocessed) are exempt. Medicines are 2%, healthcare services are 4%. All other goods and services including restaurants, hotels, clothing, electronics and utilities are at the full 13%. Internet and phone services are 13%.

🇹🇭 Thailand

Standard VAT is legally 10% but has been reduced to 7% by Royal Decree, extended to September 2026. Basic groceries, healthcare, education and residential rent are exempt. Everything else — dining, hotels, clothing, electronics, furniture — is at 7%. Internet and mobile are also 7%.

🇲🇺 Mauritius

Single VAT rate of 15% with no reduced rate. Key exemptions include basic staple foods (rice, flour, bread), healthcare and medical services, and the sale of residential property. Most food beyond basic staples, restaurants, hotels, clothing, electronics and utilities are taxed at 15%.

🇻🇳 Vietnam

Standard VAT is 10%, but a 2% reduction applies to many goods and services (making them 8%) through December 2026. Basic foods, medicines, clean water and some healthcare are at 5%. Telecom and internet remain at 10% (excluded from the reduction). Long-term residential rent is exempt.

Rates are accurate as of early 2026 and verified against official government sources and reputable tax authorities (PwC Tax Summaries, KPMG, Avalara, Stripe Tax, and national revenue authorities). Tax laws change. Always confirm current rates with a local tax professional or the relevant national tax authority before making financial decisions. Rates shown are for mainland/standard regions — autonomous zones (e.g. Portuguese Azores and Madeira, Greek Aegean islands) may apply different rates.
